Leica Cameras

Ah yes, the cream of the crop. Every photographer's dream camera is a Leica, and most particularly their rangefinders.

The company Leica itself was founded in Wetzlar, Germany circa 1849, and their first 35mm can be dated back to 1913. They're known for their superb build quality and having been in the hands of legendary artists such as Cartier-Bresson, Winnogrand, and many, many others.

I won't ever deny the fact that Leica produces some beautiful technology, but it's overhyped and overpiced by a long shot. If you want one of their digital bodies (such as the M-10), you're paying close to $10,000 just for the camera body. To add to that, you're paying that much for a camera without autofocus, little to no functionality, a sub-par sensor and a logo. In Layman's Terms, you're paying for a brick with no real world uses other than looking real special with your expensive camera and pointy shoes.
